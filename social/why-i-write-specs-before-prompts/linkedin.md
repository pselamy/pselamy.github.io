A lot of what people call agent failure is really spec failure.

Prompts are useful, but they are too lossy to be the unit of work for serious systems. Once work crosses sessions, tools, or reviewers, you need something more durable: problem, constraints, interfaces, acceptance, failure plan.

That is the shift that made AI-assisted work more reliable for me. The prompt is a request. The spec is the system.

I wrote up the full argument here: https://selamy.dev/posts/why-i-write-specs-before-prompts/

OpenAI's SWE-Lancer is a good reference point on the capability side: https://openai.com/index/swe-lancer/
